I am a PhD Student in Cognitive Neuroscience at CMU, supervised by Jonathan Tsay. I did my undergraduate studies at the University of California, Berkeley in Cognitive Science with a minor in Computer Science. My honors thesis was advised by Richard Ivry, for which I received the Robert J. Glushko Prize for Distinguished Undergraduate Research in Cognitive Science.

I am broadly interested in the intersection between cognition and action. Specifically, I use (1) computational modeling to study the formation of cognitive strategies and (2) psychophysical tasks to understand the role of time in motor memory formation. I hope to extend this work to individuals with neurological disorders (e.g., cerebellar degeneration), children, and potentially use neuroimaging methods in the future. Outside research, I enjoy playing board games with friends, reading, and experimenting in the kitchen.
