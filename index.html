Anjuli Niyogi

I am an undergraduate student studying Cognitive Science and Computer Science at the University of California, Berkeley. I am currently jointly a part of the Cognition and Action Lab (Berkeley) and the Physical Intelligence Lab (CMU), where I am advised by Richard Ivry and Jonathan Tsay.

My research focuses on the intersection of cognition and action, studying how we develop cognitive strategies to help execute our motor goals. I recently had the opportunity to present my research at the Society for Neuroscience conference this year in Chicago, IL. See my abstract here.

I am looking forward to pursuing a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience/Psychology.
